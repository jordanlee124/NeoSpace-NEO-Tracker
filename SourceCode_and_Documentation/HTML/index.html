NEOspace

Space has been a topic of interest for humanity for decades, with one of the first missions being the launch of the 'Sputnik' rover to orbit Earth by the USSR in 1957. Even recently, with the rise of companies like SpaceX and Blue Origin, large volumes of data are still being collected to cater for the continuously growing interest in space travel of the general public. The growing success of these companies shows that even today, scientists still continue to explore the vast expanse, a recent example being the successful landing of the Mars Perseverance Rover on the 18th of February this year.

What is NEOspace

NEOspace is a fully interactive interface that allows users to easily view a collection of near earth objects(NEO) at either the current time or some time in the past. NEOspace allows users to visualise the distances between earth and near Earth objects using built in tools.

How to use it

Below is a quick guide on how to use our site. It'll show you a quick summary of our pages and the different features you can access.

Home Page

Click on different elements of the page to be redirected to pages.

  • Click on the Earth to be taken to the Near Earth Object Main page.
  • Click on Mars to be taken to the Mars Rover Photo page.
  • Click on the rotating atom to be taken to the Education Page.
  • Try find the hidden easter egg on this page

    • Near-Earth Objects Page

    Scroll through 'space' to discover near-earth objects (NEOs) approaching earth today!

  • Click 'Earth' to view the Astronomy Picture of the Day.
  • Click on a specific NEO to view information about it.

    • Personalise your search using the filters on the left.

  • Choose to view NEOs at a particular date or a range.
  • Enter a known id of a NEO to learn more about it.
  • View only the NEOs that orbit a specific planet.
  • Choose only hazardous or sentry NEOs from today.

    • Mars Rovers Page

    Click on the different tabs 'Curiosity', 'Spirit' and 'Opportunity' to see photos from each rover

    Click on the hamburger icon to access the filters. You can search by:

  • Photos taken at a particular date.
  • By a particular camera.
  • By the number of Martian days since the rover landed.

    Connect with other users and upload space-themed image!

    Click on the images to increase their popularity, see if you can get your favourite to the top!
